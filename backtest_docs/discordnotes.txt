the Confidence Interval Strategy.
Executive Summary
The core philosophy of this strategy is Dynamic Risk Management. Instead of risking the same amount on every single trade, you adjust your leverage (position size) and your trade management rules based on how confident you are in the setup. This allows a trader to experiment and "forward test" with real money without blowing an account, while maximizing profits on "A+" setups.
1. The Core Framework: The Confidence Scale
The speaker uses a 50k Prop Account as the baseline for these calculations. The strategy requires you to rate every potential trade on a scale of 1 to 10 before entering.
Tier 1: Low Confidence (The "Testing" Phase)
Rating: 1 to 3
Use Case: You are testing a new strategy, gathering information, or forward-testing a setup where you want "skin in the game" to feel the psychology, but the technicals are not perfect.
Position Size: 1 to 2 Micros.
Risk Profile: extremely low risk. Do not be an "idiot" and risk full leverage here.
Expectations: Do not expect a home run (500+ ticks). You are looking for a quick scalp or confirmation.
Target (R:R): 1:2 Risk-to-Reward ratio.
Tier 2: Medium Confidence (The "Standard" Trade)
Rating: 4 to 6
Use Case: A solid structural trade. It has good confluence, but it isn't perfect. It might be a "B" or "C" grade setup. You believe it will work, but you aren't willing to bet the house on it.
Position Size: 3 to 6 Micros.
Target (R:R): 1:3 or 1:4 Risk-to-Reward ratio.
Tier 3: High Confidence (The "A+" Setup)
Rating: 7 to 10
Use Case: The "A+" setup. High confluence, aligns with your best strategy, and everything suggests it will go your way. If this trade fails, itâ€™s simply a probability outlier.
Position Size: 7 to 10 Micros (or 1â€“2 Minis).
Target (R:R): 1:4 Risk-to-Reward ratio plus runners.
2. Trade Management Rules
The strategy changes how you manage the trade (Break Even & Take Profit) based on the tier.
The Universal Rule: Every trade uses a 50-tick Stop Loss.
Break Even (BE) Rules:
Low Confidence: Move to Break Even quickly.
Trigger: When the trade is up 50 to 65 ticks.
Why: You don't trust this trade fully. If it moves a bit and comes back, get out. Don't let a test trade become a loss.
High Confidence: Move to Break Even slowly.
Trigger: When the trade is up 100 ticks.
Why: You trust the setup. You need to give an A+ setup "breathing room" to fluctuate without wicked you out before the big move.
Take Profit (TP) & Runners:
Low/Medium Confidence: Hard exits. Hit your 1:2 or 1:3 target and close the full position.
High Confidence: Scale out and leave runners.
Execution: At the 1:4 target, close 30% to 50% of the position (e.g., if holding 10 micros, close 3-5 of them).
Runner Management: Leave the remaining micros to run to significantly higher targets, while locking the Stop Loss in profit to ensure the trade is a winner regardless of what happens next.
3. Implementation Checklist
To implement this tomorrow, follow this flowchart for every trade alert:
Spot the Setup: Identify a potential entry.
Rate the Setup (1-10):
Is this an experiment? -> Low (1-3)
Is this a standard decent setup? -> Medium (4-6)
Is this an A+ snipe? -> High (7-10)
Size the Position:
Low: 1-2 Micros.
Med: 3-6 Micros.
High: 7-10 Micros.
Set the Bracket:
Stop Loss: 50 Ticks (Fixed).
Take Profit: Adjust based on tier (1:2 for low, 1:4+ for high).
Manage the Trade:
If Low Confidence: Move Stop to Break Even as soon as you see +50 ticks.
If High Confidence: Wait for +100 ticks before moving Stop to Break Even.
Why use this strategy?
Prevents Blowouts: You will never blow an account testing a new theory because you are only using 1 micro.
Psychological Data: Forward testing with 1 micro is better than "paper trading" because you still feel real emotions (Greed/Fear), but the financial damage is negligible.
Maximizes Winners: By going heavy only on A+ setups and letting runners ride, one big win can cover many small "testing" losses.
